I. VISION
          A resource center committed to provide quality service and information on Philippine history through its extensive collection of resource materials available in print, electronic and digital format.

II. MISSION
          The National Historical Institute Resource Center shall identify, select, acquire and preserve materials in any format concerning Philippine history, arts, culture and other related topics. The Resource Center will continue to serve efficiently, making the materials readily available through computerized systems.

III. OBJECTIVES

 

  • To acquire books and other resource materials not only print but also electronic and digital to enhance and update the existing collection;
  • To meet the growing and advancing research, library and information needs of the clientele;
  • To stimulate and support further the research interests of the NHI community; and
  • To provide resource materials that will enable the researchers expand their knowledge and make them grow intellectually.

 

IV. SERVICE AND USERS GUIDELINES

          A. USER’S PRIORITIZATION

                   Users of the NHI Resource Center shall be prioritized accordingly:

  • NHI Researchers
  • Researchers from government offices
  • History scholars and graduate school students
  • College students

 

The center shall accommodate requests for assistance from government offices and private entities upon presentation of an official request from the head of the concerned office.

B. RULES AND REGULATIONS
         

  • The Resource Center is open from 8:00 – 5:00 with a lunch break from 12:00 noon to 1:00. Outside researchers must finish their work by 4:30 pm to allow the librarians to return the library materials to their proper places.
  • The librarian shall keep a record of all books and materials borrowed by the researchers. These shall be inspected for missing pages and any form of vandalism upon return.
  • Outside users shall not be allowed to bring their bags inside the Resource Center. Bags shall be deposited in the guard’s counter.
  • NHI employees shall be allowed to borrow books from the Center for private reading. However, these must be returned at the end of the week. If the materials are not returned the librarian will duly notify them.
  • Newspapers and other periodical shall not be taken out of the Center
  • Photocopying of library materials shall be discouraged (government agencies and history scholars may be allowed). However, taking photos through a digital camera may be allowed provided a written request for such is approved by the Chief of the RPHD.
  • Smoking, eating, chatting and sleeping inside the Center is not allowed. Those engaged in these activities shall be asked to leave the Center
  • The Resource Center exists primarily for the Institute. The Center may be closed to serve as a venue for important activities and outside researchers shall be asked to leave. The Institute security force shall be asked to stop the coming of outside users whenever necessary.
