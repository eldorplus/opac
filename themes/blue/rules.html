LENDING POLICIES

A. Who may borrow books?

A.1 FOR OVERNIGHT AND HOME READING

  1. Incumbent Senators.
  2. Duly registered borrowers of the Senators’ staff with proper Senate identification and borrower’s card.*
  3. Officers and employees of the Secretariat with proper Senate identification and borrower’s card.

* Duly registered borrowers are the names of Senators' staff submitted to the library for its
Roster of Registered Borrowers.

A.2 FOR ROOM USE

  • All those who have overnight and home reading privilege (A.1 a-c)
  • "Non-Senate" researchers whose research work will be limited to the use of Senate publications, e.g. Official compilations of laws; Journals and Records of the Senate; and the like.

 

B. What materials may be borrowed?

B.1 FOR OVERNIGHT AND HOME READING

Library books and other publications may be taken out or borrowed except those enumerated under B.2

B.2 FOR LIBRARY USE ONLY

  • Reference materials (marked "R" on spine)
  • A-V collection
  • Periodicals / journals
  • Vertical File / news clippings
  • Books and other publications marked "NOT TO BE TAKEN OUT"
  • Single-copy books

C. How many materials may be borrowed and for how long?

C.1 A maximum of three (3) books are allowed to be taken out at a time for a period of five (5) days.
C.2 Loaned materials may be renewed for another three (3) days unless requested for by another clientele.
C.3 Loaned materials are subject to recall if urgently needed by another borrower.
